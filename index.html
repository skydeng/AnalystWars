Analyst Wars

Welcome to Analyst Wars, a social network of amateur analysts competing against Wall Street professionals. In today’s financial industry analysts, brokerage firms and investment banks don’t have small investors best interest in mind when rating stocks. Therefore, the people have the power to decide stock pricing and should exercise that right. We offer the opportunity to create your own stock ratings on a weekly, monthly and quarterly basis. The top analysts for each stock will be rewarded with gift cards, promotional deals and vacations.

“Imagine Analysts Ratings Network, HedgeChatter and Estimize had a child with the StockWars iOS app and Thinknum raised it; a social network of amateur stock analysts competing against Wall Street firms and being rewarded based on performance; interface includes profile, leader board, aggregated stock pricing charts and the opportunity to beat professional analysts.” ~ Timothy Hooker

Stock Rating System

  1. Create analyst profile, username, age, email, location, education, bio, picture, investment experience and sector/market cap research expertise.
  2. Begin creating your own stock ratings Buy, Hold, Sell Enter stock price estimates for week, month or quarterly dates specified by Analyst Wars (Ratings close each Friday for the following week and the last day of each month or quarter for upcoming ratings)
  3. Track your performance on a leaderboard competing against professional analyst calls (includes average percent price over/under estimates, number of stocks rated, open ratings, closed ratings and W-L’s)
  4. Earn rewards for being the top analyst of each sector (able to estimate ETF’s and equities, in order to be a sector analyst one must have over 5 ratings in the sector)

Charting System

  1. User enters stock rating and price estimate
  2. Data is collected and aggregated into chart with other users ratings to create AnalystWars Consensus compared to Wall Street Consensus
    3. ~ The consensus charting will be valuable to individual investors seeking an overall picture of where firms and other smaller investors think the ETF or Stock will be headed.

Our goal is to develop a product that helps investors navigate trades with better accuracy and use a social consensus to interpret future stock prices that compare against Wall Street Analysts. “Analyst Wars could be cool if you were able to group all the analysts offering coverage on a stock and compare their findings.” Says potential customer and trader Matt Uhrin, a CPA and Graduate student at Michigan State University studying Finance. We plan to cover only successful companies with outstanding financial balance sheets and diverage away from speculative stocks that so many traders like to make quick profits in. True investors buy into strong companies that have undervalued prices like Warren Buffett and Benjamin Graham. Our mission is to use that philosophy to predict future stock prices in companies with strong fundamentals.
